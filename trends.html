Trends:

Nail polish trends from the 20th century until now

In the 1900's Camille Clifford, best known as 'the Original Gibson Girl' due to her chingnon'd hair and S shaped corset had her own signature nails. Her nails were short in length, neat and shiny from a clear nail enamel.

In the 1920's Josephine Baker, well known as one of the first internationally recognised African-American showgirls, stayed true to the trends within the flapper community. She wore either pale pink or red and kept her nails oval shaped with no color on her half moon.

Starting around 1925, nail polish was commonly painted in a 'half moon' style. Nail tips and the moons near the cuticle were not painted to follow the style.

In the 1930's Jean Harlow was best known as the original Blonde Bombshell, she starred in several movies and kept her nails oval, painted red with the half moon exposed.

In the 1940's Betty Grable was best known as the iconic pin-up girl for American troops fighting in WWII. Her nails were painted a bright cherry red that were longer and close to an almond shape.

In the 1950's Grace Kelly was best known as a timeless Hollywood actress who became Princess of Monaco after marrying Prince Ranier. Her nails were painted in more nutral colors like a pale peachy pink and were almond shape. Because her nails were more simplistic, her nails were copied by the everyday woman during this time period.

In the 1960's Jean Shrimpton was best known as one of the first supermodels, she was the darling of `60's fashion and beauty brands. Her nails were normally nude or tan and she had pointed tips.

In the 1970's Cher is best known for her music career. She started as half of the musical duo Sonny and Cher until she became a solo superstar. Her nails were painted white or in the french tip style and she had long squoval nails.

In the 1980's Grace Jones is best known as one of the pioneers of 80's fashion. She had long square nails that were a contrast to the nude nails of other 80's icons.

In the 1990's Gwen Stefani is best known as the front woman of the band No Doubt until she started her solo career. She was seen as the anti-Britney and had a girly but punk fashion and nails.
